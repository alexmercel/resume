# Cover Letter Generation Rules

Follow these rules strictly whenever generating a new Cover Letter based on a Target Job Description and Candidate Experience:

1. **Length Constraint**: The cover letter must be strictly around 300 to 400 words. Do NOT generate overly wordy or repetitive documents.
2. **First-Person & Identity**: It MUST be written in the first person ("I"). You MUST use the Candidate's actual Name, Email, Phone, and Location from `profile.md`. You MUST include the current date formatted appropriately at the top.
3. **Tone & Style**: Make the letter sound highly human, genuine, and authentic. Actively AVOID using overly generic professional buzzwords ("synergy," "delve," "testament," "unparalleled") or standard heavy AI-like phrasing. The tone should be natural and engaging.
4. **Fit & Justification**: Provide a clear, compelling reason why the candidate as a person is the PERFECT fit for the specific role. Synthesize the candidate's previous experience and connect it logically to the things the company is doing or needs. Focus on *how* the previous experience makes them a highly suitable candidate for the JD.
5. **No Placeholders Constraints**: The output MUST be completely ready out of the box. Do NOT use any [Placeholders], [Insert Company Name Here], or blank fields. If specific information (like the exact Hiring Manager name) is not present in the Job Description, simply omit that line entirely and use a general professional greeting (e.g., "Dear Hiring Manager," or "Dear [Company] Hiring Team,").
6. **Formatting Limiters**: Return the document as plain readable text with logical paragraph breaks. Do NOT inject markdown markdown wrappers like ` ```text `. Just output the raw words.
